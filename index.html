How to Learn

Approach to Learning

📋
01 / 06

Build a Strong Study Plan

Use the provided example schedule linking overarching principles with specialty topics. Adapt it based on your rotations. Don't rely on last-minute revision — there's too much to cover.

🏥
02 / 06

Create Learning Opportunities

Use the 'On the Wards' objectives to guide you through key competencies. Go into ward rounds with purpose — set daily goals like interpreting AXRs or understanding diabetes management.

📝
03 / 06

Use WBAs as Learning Tools

Long cases are case-based learning opportunities. Use handover WBAs for microlearning and viva practice. The viva requires practising clinical history, interpretation, and management plans.

💻
04 / 06

Use Canvas Resources Wisely

Online resources supplement ward-based learning. Listen to the 'Unscrubbed Podcasts' during your commute. Textbooks are available as ebooks — review highlighted high-yield chapters.

🎓
05 / 06

Maximise Clinical Tutorials

Clinical Scenario Tutorials provide deep discussion of surgical management and overarching principles. Bedside tutorials are your chance to practise viva skills with experienced tutors.

🤖
06 / 06

AI & Web Resources

Flash cards help with rote learning but not clinical reasoning. AI tools can be helpful but use cautiously. Geeky Medics, TeachMeSurgery, and eMedici are excellent supplements. SUMS has an institutional login trial for 2026.

Curriculum Content

Surgical Specialties

Vascular & Cardiothoracic Surgery

Curriculum Topics
⚡ Acute Surgical Conditions
  • Ruptured AAA
  • Acute Aortic Syndromes (dissection, penetrating ulcer)
  • Acute Limb Ischaemia
  • Deep venous thrombosis
Vascular Diseases
  • Peripheral artery disease (intermittent claudication, CLTI)
  • Aortic aneurysm
  • Diabetes-related foot disease (ulcers, sepsis, osteomyelitis)
  • Venous disease (superficial, varicose, ulcers, DVT)
  • Carotid stenosis
Cardiothoracic
  • Surgical approaches to ischaemic heart disease
  • Valvular surgery
  • Pneumothorax
  • Lung cancer
  • Lung abscess / empyema

On the Wards

Clinical Competencies
Patients with leg pain ▾
Differentiate between acute and chronic arterial ischaemia, venous insufficiency, neurological and musculoskeletal causes.
Lower limb swelling ▾
Recognise signs/symptoms of venous insufficiency, venous thrombosis, and metabolic causes of peripheral oedema.
Lower limb / foot ulceration ▾
Identify arterial, venous and neuroischaemic ulcers; recognise superimposed infection with cellulitis or osteomyelitis; initiate appropriate wound care; when surgery is indicated.
Aortic aneurysm or dissection ▾
Recognise acute symptomatic and asymptomatic presentations; screening and investigation; thresholds for treatment; common perioperative management issues.
Ischaemic heart disease / coronary surgery ▾
Potential graft conduits; pre-surgical workup and investigations.

Orthopaedics, Hand Surgery, Plastics & Burns

Curriculum Topics
⚡ Acute Surgical Conditions
  • Neck of femur fracture
  • Acute extremity fracture
  • Acute lacerations and tendon injuries
  • Wound closure
Orthopaedic Conditions
  • Surgical management of osteoarthritis (hip and knee)
  • Common shoulder conditions (incl. dislocation)
  • Bone and joint infections (septic joint, osteomyelitis)
  • Fracture complications (non-union, nerve injuries, compartment syndrome)
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome
  • Hand injuries, lacerations, tendon disruption
Plastics & Burns
  • Common reconstructive surgical techniques
  • Wound healing
  • Skin cancers (SCC/BCC, Melanoma)
  • Benign subcutaneous lumps (lipoma, sebaceous cysts, ganglion)
  • Subcutaneous abscesses
  • Soft tissue tumours including sarcomas
  • Acute and longer-term burns management

On the Wards

Clinical Competencies
Interpret fracture / dislocation x-ray ▾
Describe relevant anatomy, potential complications, and morphology of the fracture.
Complications of orthopaedic trauma ▾
Compartment syndrome, nerve and vessel injury, open fracture, haemarthrosis, infections such as osteomyelitis.
Hand, hip, knee, shoulder examination ▾
Including relevant anatomy and functional deficits for each joint.
Recovery after joint replacement ▾
VTE prevention, mobilisation, multidisciplinary rehabilitation including orthogeriatrics.
Benign vs malignant skin lesions ▾
Recognise melanoma; describe surgical approaches and safe margins; recommend appropriate wound management.
Skin grafting methods ▾
Split skin, full-thickness, flaps, and synthetic grafts.

Curriculum Topics

  • Acute appendicitis
  • Bowel obstruction (small and large)
  • Bowel ischaemia (incl. mesenteric ischaemia)
  • Sigmoid volvulus
Colorectal Conditions
  • Colon and rectal cancer (and benign polyps)
  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Diverticular disease and complications
  • Anorectal disease (fissures, abscesses, haemorrhoids)

On the Wards

Clinical Competencies
Assessment of acute abdominal pain ▾
Clinical history and physical examination; role of investigations; key differentiating symptoms/signs; indications for surgery.
Patient with acute peritonitis ▾
Recognise clinical signs, differential diagnoses; acute management including pain relief; role of investigations and surgical interventions.
Per rectal bleeding ▾
Recognise causes of chronic and large volume bleeding; acute management and investigations; red flags.
Non-surgical management of bowel obstruction ▾
Fluid resuscitation/replacement and electrolyte management; quantifying fluid losses; when to consider surgical intervention.
Surgical treatment of bowel cancer ▾
Different operations performed; screening and surveillance; ERAS pathways.

Upper GI Surgery

Curriculum Topics
⚡ Acute Surgical Conditions
  • Acute pancreatitis
  • Acute cholecystitis / cholangitis
  • Peptic ulcer perforation and bleeding
  • Incarcerated / strangulated hernia
GI / Pancreatic / Liver
  • Oesophageal stricture
  • Oesophageal and gastric cancer (incl. Barrett's)
  • Pancreatic cancer
  • Chronic pancreatitis (incl. pseudocysts)
  • Cholelithiasis and choledocholithiasis
  • Cholecystitis, cholangitis
  • Cholangiocarcinoma
  • Benign and malignant liver tumours
Hernias
  • Femoral · Inguinal (direct and indirect)
  • Abdominal wall hernia
  • Hiatus hernias

On the Wards

Clinical Competencies
Interpret abdominal/chest x-rays ▾
Recognise common pathology such as perforated viscus and bowel obstruction.
Acute cholecystitis / cholangitis ▾
Role of antimicrobials; investigations; gall bladder surgery indications and timing.
Obstructive jaundice ▾
Clinical history and physical examination; interpreting LFTs, coagulation/INR; ordering appropriate imaging.
Pancreatitis ▾
Key risk factors; fluid resuscitation; acute pain management; staging/severity; longer term sequalae.
Groin hernia ▾
Recognise anatomical site/location; identify complications including strangulation/incarceration; consider indication and timing of surgical options.

Urology & Transplant Surgery

Curriculum Topics
⚡ Acute Surgical Conditions
  • Renal colic (urolithiasis)
  • Urinary obstruction and hydronephrosis
  • Testicular torsion
Prostate, Testes & Scrotum
  • Prostate cancer
  • Benign prostatic hypertrophy
  • Hydrocele / epididymitis / varicocele
  • Testicular cancer
Kidney, Bladder & Voiding
  • Urinary incontinence and obstruction
  • Urethral stricture
  • Bladder cancer · Renal cancer · Renal calculi
  • Adrenal masses
Transplant Surgery
  • Solid organ transplantation (kidney, liver, pancreas)
  • Organ donation and matching

On the Wards

Clinical Competencies
Renal colic ▾
Diagnosis and imaging modalities; acute pain management; recognising complications including when ureteric decompression (stenting/nephrostomy) is indicated.
Urinary retention and catheters ▾
Interpreting bladder scans; indications for catheterisation; complications such as haematuria, clots, infection.
Prostate cancer treatments ▾
Surgical and non-surgical treatments; screening and surveillance; complications such as voiding and erectile dysfunction.
Voiding dysfunction ▾
Differentiating between obstructive and overactive causes of incontinence.
Transplant patient management ▾
Risks associated with immunosuppression; options and pathways in organ donation (cadaveric vs living donors).

Curriculum Topics

  • Traumatic brain injury
  • Burns

Neurosurgery
  • Cerebral aneurysms
  • Brain tumours (glioma, meningioma, GBM) and metastases
  • Surgery for chronic back pain
Trauma Surgery
  • Trauma-related pneumohaemothorax
  • Solid organ laceration and intra-abdominal bleeding

On the Wards

Clinical Competencies
Assessment of a trauma patient ▾
Primary, secondary and tertiary surveys; c-spine precautions; GCS assessment.
Postoperative decreased LOC ▾
Differentiate between neurosurgical causes (bleeding, raised ICP) and other causes such as stroke and delirium.
Interpret a CT Brain ▾
Identify bleeding and solid masses; differentiate between subdural, extradural and intracranial bleeding.
Spinal surgery indications for back pain ▾
Recognise red flags; discuss alternative treatment options and pain management.

Perioperative Care

Overarching Principles of Surgery

Pre-operative Assessment & Risk

  • Identification of the high-risk surgical patient
  • ASA classification and perioperative risk
  • Approaches to pre-operative investigations
  • Diabetes management in perioperative setting
  • Antithrombotic management perioperatively
  • Management of chronic pain patients (incl. opioids)

Preparation for Surgery

  • Perioperative fasting (ERAS protocols, Sip-til-send)
  • VTE prophylaxis
  • Surgical antimicrobial prophylaxis
  • Theatre preparation, informed consent, emergency bookings

Intraoperative Aspects

  • Surgical safety checklist and communication in theatre
  • Aseptic techniques and sterility
  • Local anaesthetic doses and administration
  • Surgical approaches and techniques for wound closure

Post-operative Management

  • Pain management (acute and chronic)
  • IV fluid management (resuscitation, replacement, maintenance)
  • Common perioperative complications (HAP, MI, delirium, ileus, DVT/PE)
  • Fever in post-operative patients
  • Postoperative anaemia and bleeding
  • Perioperative nutrition
  • Rehabilitation, mobility and function after surgery

On the Wards

  • Charting IV fluids for surgical patients
  • Management of postoperative pain (escalation, modalities)
  • Patient with postoperative fever (source, investigations, antimicrobials)
  • Postoperative anaemia (active bleeding vs losses, transfusion)
  • Assessment of a surgical wound (infection, haematoma, dehiscence)
  • Handover before and after surgery
  • Preparing a patient for discharge after surgery

  • Week 4 — Bleeding and breathing after surgery
  • Week 5 — Anticoagulants in surgery
